## Design Context

### Users
Residents of Novozybkov, Russia (small city, ~30k people) — parents registering their children (7–12, 13–17), working adults wanting AI productivity skills, and entrepreneurs. Context: evening or weekend browsing, on phones or mid-range laptops. Job: understand what the school offers, trust the instructor, and register. Trust is the primary conversion barrier — users are skeptical that "AI courses" are scams.

### Brand Personality
**Warm, hands-on, local.** Three words: **grounded · capable · modern**. Not a faceless tech corp — it's one real person (Denis Stepan) teaching in a physical classroom. The amber color feels earned and warm, not cool-tech gimmicky.

### Aesthetic Direction
Dark base (evening study context), amber/gold accent as primary brand signal. Neural network visualization in hero is on-brand. Should feel like a serious local institution that happens to be visually distinctive — not Silicon Valley startup, not generic Russian IT school. Anti-references: Skillbox/Geekbrains (corporate template), generic SaaS landing pages with gradient purple.

### Design Principles
1. **Trust through specificity** — real address, real photo, real prices, real instructor name. No vague claims.
2. **Mobile-first conversion** — most users will first see this on a phone. The primary CTA must be thumb-reachable.
3. **Warm dark** — dark background earns its place (evening context), but amber accents must feel warm, not cold/cyber.
4. **Restraint on effects** — glassmorphism and gradient text undermine the "serious local school" credibility signal. Use sparingly.
5. **Motion serves communication** — animate to reveal information, not to decorate.
